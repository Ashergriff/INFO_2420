Web Site Critiques

Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Pitchfork - https://pitchfork.com/

  1. My first impression of this site was that it felt very cluttered and not very organized. There is a lot of content on the homepage, and it makes the page feel busy.
  2. The site uses a news-style layout with major headlines or featured stories in the middle and additional stories placed around the sides. This makes it feel like an online magazine or news page.
  3. The structure does not work. I can see what the designers were trying to do, but the proportions they chose make the page feel too busy and complicated. In the end Im not sure where to look.
  4. I think the graphics, layout, and media add to the mess on the main page. Even though the images are ment to get a click, there is so much happening at once that it becomes overwhelming.
  5. The site loaded pretty fast on my device. Since there is a lot of content on the homepage, I thought the load time was very reasonable.
  6. The text was easy to read because there is strong contrast, with mostly black text on a white background. The simple color contrast helps readability and does not make the page harder to use.
  7. The site has a menu at the top with classic navigation links. I actually think the navigation is one of the better parts of the site because it is clear and easy to follow.
  8. The site has a repeated look and feel across the page, with a white background, black text, and red highlights. That color scheme helps give the site a feeling of concistancy.
  9. The site is fairly responsive when the window gets smaller. One thing that seemed strange to me is that the layout feels limited at larger sizes, almost like it stops scaling as well as it could.
  10. One thing this site does well is the menu and title area across the top. The small amount of red draws attention to the subscribe area, and that part of the page is very readable.
